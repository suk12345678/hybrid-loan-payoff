why even compare strategies?

the difference between strategies might be:

  • HUGE — if you got high-interest debt, avalanche could save you literal thousands
  • minimal — if your interest rates are all similar, the cost difference is like $200. not worth stressing over.
  • somewhere in between — a hybrid approach might give you 90% of the savings with way better motivation

you won't know until you run YOUR numbers. not some example. yours.

building a custom hybrid strategy

the calculator lets you drag and drop your loans into whatever order you want. it's actually kinda fun.

example hybrid strategies to try

  • quick win first: pay off smallest loan, THEN switch to avalanche
  • high-interest with breaks: tackle highest-interest, then knock out a small one for motivation, then back to high-interest
  • balanced: alternate between small balances and high interest

what if the numbers surprise you?

sometimes the "optimal" strategy isn't what you expect. here's what i've seen:

common surprises

  • "avalanche only saves me $200?" — if your interest rates are close, snowball's psychological benefits are 100% worth the small cost
  • "snowball costs me $2,000 more?" — if you got high-interest debt, avalanche is clearly better. the math doesn't lie.
  • "a hybrid is almost as good as avalanche?" — YES. you can get 90% of the savings with way better motivation. this is the sweet spot.
